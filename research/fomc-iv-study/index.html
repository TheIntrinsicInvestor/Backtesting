01Study Design

Why FOMC meetings create a predictable options market cycle

Federal Reserve rate decisions are the single most anticipated scheduled macro events in global markets. The options market prices this anticipated uncertainty as a risk premium in the days before each meeting, then rapidly resets once the uncertainty resolves. The question is whether that pattern is systematic, how much it varies by decision type and communication surprise, and whether the IV build-up is actually large enough to exploit with short straddles on SPY.

We track three implied volatility instruments across 55 FOMC meetings from 2018 to 2024: SPX 30-day ATM IV from OptionMetrics (delta=50, days=30), TLT 30-day ATM IV (representing rates market uncertainty), and the CBOE VIX (pulled from FRED). Each meeting is classified on two dimensions.

Two-layer classification. Primary: decision type (Hike, Hold, or Cut) based on the actual rate change. Secondary: communication surprise (Hawkish, Neutral, or Dovish) based on dot plot changes, statement language, and same-day market reaction. The secondary layer captures what actually moves markets: many in-line rate decisions carry large guidance surprises.

02IV Event Profile

SPX implied vol builds in the final week before FOMC, then crushes on announcement day

Each meeting's IV series is normalised so that the mean of T-20 to T-15 equals 100, then averaged across all 55 meetings (excluding the three COVID outliers). The pattern is consistent: a gradual build in implied vol over the 5 to 10 days preceding the announcement, followed by a sharp collapse on or immediately after the decision day.

SPX and VIX show the most pronounced pre-meeting premium, consistent with equity market uncertainty dominating options pricing around rate decisions. TLT IV tends to peak earlier, reflecting that rates market participants price in the decision further in advance.

03Surprise Analysis

Hold meetings carry the largest pre-meeting IV premium, with communication tone shaping divergent post-announcement paths

Splitting the IV profile by decision type reveals that hold meetings generate the largest pre-meeting IV build-up, with normalised IV reaching 105.8 at T-1, against 102.1 for hike meetings. The gap likely reflects the Hold category spanning both high-uncertainty regimes (hawkish pivots, ZLB-to-tightening transitions) and routine holds across the full 2018 to 2024 period. Cut meetings are harder to interpret given the small sample and structural mix (2019 mid-cycle easing vs. 2024 normalisation vs. 2020 emergency actions).

SPX IV profile by decision type (Hike / Hold / Cut), normalised
Hike (n=15) Hold (n=34) Cut (n=6)

Within hold meetings (n=34), communication surprises drive material differences in IV dynamics. Hawkish guidance meetings (n=8) enter the announcement with pre-elevated IV near 118 at T-1, reflecting markets pricing in additional uncertainty before a hawkish pivot. That premium decays gradually post-announcement, settling near 105 at T+10, still above baseline. Neutral (n=16) and Dovish (n=10) meetings show more subdued pre-meeting IV, but both exhibit a pronounced delayed spike around T+3, peaking at 118 and 114 respectively. Dovish meetings ultimately fall well below baseline by T+10 (92.8), while Neutral holds above it (106.6). These subgroup samples are small (n=8 to 16) and limit confident inference on individual paths.

04Strategy A: Pre-Meeting Straddle Sell

Selling the pre-meeting premium: enter T-1, exit T+1

The pre-meeting IV build-up creates a natural opportunity for short-vol traders. Strategy A sells an ATM SPY straddle at the close on T-1 (the last trading day before the announcement) and buys it back at the close on T+1. The position is short vega and short gamma, profiting when IV collapses post-announcement and the underlying does not move far enough to overcome the premium collected.

All P&L figures are gross of transaction costs. SPY straddle bid-ask spreads typically run $0.05 to $0.15 per leg, or $10 to $30 per contract round-trip, which represents a meaningful drag at these premium levels.

What these results mean

Strategy A fails on every meaningful metric. The 51% win rate (55 non-COVID meetings) is statistically indistinguishable from a coin flip, and the annualised Sharpe is deeply negative at -0.76. The strategy bleeds steadily: cumulative P&L reaches -$3,496 over the sample period, driven by outsized losses on Dovish and Hike meetings that more than offset any gains from quiet Hold meetings.

Disaggregating by decision type shows no viable cohort. Hike meetings (n=15) average -$71 per contract and produce 40% win rate as sharp post-announcement moves overwhelm the collected premium. Hold meetings with Dovish communication (n=10) are the worst cohort: 30% win rate and avg -$177. Even the best-looking subgroup, Hold/Neutral (n=17, 71% win rate, avg +$35), offers no practical edge once implementation costs are applied.

Strategy A should not be carried out. Even setting aside the negative aggregate Sharpe, the only subgroup that showed a positive return (Hold/Neutral, +$35 avg gross) is operationally inaccessible: communication tone is confirmed only after the FOMC statement is released, so it is impossible to pre-classify a meeting as Neutral before entering the trade. The +$35 gross average is also fully consumed by round-trip bid-ask costs of $10 to $30 per contract. There is no systematic, repeatable edge in this strategy after realistic transaction costs.

Disclaimer. All P&L is gross. Actual realised returns will be lower after bid-ask spread ($10 to $30 per contract round-trip), commissions, and margin costs for short options. The strategy carries unbounded gamma risk if the underlying gaps through the strike. No delta hedging is applied. These results are a backtested approximation, not a live trading record.

What these results mean

Strategy B fails in the current environment. The headline T+5 win rate of 69% and positive avg P&L of +$40 are entirely a product of the 2022 to 2023 hiking cycle. Hike meetings (n=15) account for virtually all the strategy's historical edge: 87% win rate, avg +$119 per contract, Sharpe 2.75. Remove those 15 meetings and the remaining 40 trades produce near-breakeven or negative results. Hold meetings (n=34, now the dominant regime) average -$11 per contract with a negative Sharpe. Cut meetings produced a fluke positive average over only n=6 trades, carrying no inferential weight.

The mechanism for the hike-cycle edge was intuitive but regime-specific. During the 2022 to 2023 campaign, each meeting resolved acute uncertainty about the terminal rate, and IV deflated steadily over the T+1 to T+5 window as that uncertainty cleared. That mechanism does not operate in a Hold regime: with rates unchanged and guidance incremental, there is no discrete vol event for the market to resolve after the announcement. The post-meeting IV window becomes directionless and the straddle collects insufficient premium to overcome bid-ask drag.

Strategy B should not be carried out in the current environment. As of 2025, the Fed is in a hold and gradual-cut stance. The operative regime is Hold meetings, which have produced avg -$11 per contract before transaction costs. There is no systematic edge available. Any future application would require a confirmed and sustained hiking cycle, and even in that context the 15-meeting hike sample is too small to project with confidence into the next cycle.

07Conclusions

Four takeaways from a systematic FOMC options study

①
The pre-meeting IV build-up is real and consistent. SPX implied vol rises systematically in the 5 to 10 trading days before FOMC announcements, normalising to a peak at or just before T=0. The pattern holds across hike, hold, and cut meetings, though the magnitude varies by rate cycle era.
②
The IV crush is reliable but not uniform. Hawkish Hold meetings enter the announcement with pre-elevated IV that decays slowly rather than crushing, limiting short-vol profitability. Neutral and Dovish Hold meetings show a pronounced delayed IV spike around T+3 before mean-reverting, rather than a clean immediate crush.
③
Communication surprises matter more than rate decisions. Within hold meetings, the communication surprise layer (hawkish vs. dovish dot plot or statement) drives larger divergences in IV behaviour than the rate decision itself. The Fed's forward guidance regime has effectively made the rate decision a secondary variable.
④
Neither straddle strategy offers a viable edge after realistic costs. Strategy A produces a 51% win rate and negative Sharpe in aggregate. Strategy B's positive headline is driven entirely by the 2022 to 2023 hiking cycle (n=15 meetings). In the current Hold regime, both strategies lose money before transaction costs. These strategies should not be carried out.

Limitations and what this study cannot claim

The total sample of 55 meetings limits statistical precision. Subgroup analyses (e.g., hawkish hold meetings, n=8) have insufficient power for robust inference. The 95% confidence interval on the Sharpe ratio is approximately plus or minus 0.55 at this sample size, meaning the point estimates are directionally informative but not decision-grade.

The 2018 to 2021 zero-rate era and the 2022 to 2024 hiking cycle are structurally different vol regimes. The pre-meeting IV premium was compressed during the ZIRP era and amplified during the hiking cycle. Aggregating across both periods may obscure regime-specific dynamics that a practitioner would need to account for.

Regime caveat. With the Fed's 2025 forward guidance pointing to a prolonged plateau, the 2024 cutting cycle pattern may not repeat. Studies using historical FOMC data should not be extrapolated into structurally novel rate environments without recalibration.
